Layer 4.3 — Actor Registry 

Actor Type 
Burst Actor, Cascade Actor, Drift Actor, Vacuum Actor, Ceiling‑Test Actor, Compression Actor, Purge Actor, Weaver Actor, Inversion Actor, Repair Actor, Anchor Actor, Migration Actor.

Actor Purpose (Compact Definitions)
Burst Actor: Creates sudden expansion bursts and tests upper‑lane boundaries.
Cascade Actor: Drives high‑velocity multi‑lane cascades through the corridor stack.
Drift Actor: Maintains drift corridors and forms stable drift floors.
Vacuum Actor: Creates hollow‑book spikes and sudden displacement events.
Ceiling‑Test Actor: Repeatedly taps the top boundary to test rejection or trigger migration.
Compression Actor: Tightens spacing and prepares drift → expansion transitions.
Purge Actor: Clears stacked lows/highs to reset geometry and remove stale inheritance.
Weaver Actor: Stitches lanes, maintains corridor spacing, and keeps the engine structurally alive.
Inversion Actor: Creates chaotic spacing and deep taps during geometry instability.
Repair Actor: Performs controlled descent to rebalance geometry after ceiling migration.
Anchor Actor: Establishes meaningful highs/lows that define the current family’s geometry.
Migration Actor: Shifts ceiling or floor boundaries to new structural zones.




Actor Type: Burst Actor
Purpose / Role: Creates sudden expansion bursts and tests upper‑lane boundaries.

Core Mechanics:
Triggers rapid expansion from Lane B → Lane C → Lane D
Uses burst inheritance to propagate upward movement
Breaks spacing compression temporarily
Forces geometry tests at upper boundaries
Creates short‑lived high‑velocity displacement
Often initiates cascade‑actor involvement

Lane / Sector Scope:
Primary lanes: Lane B, Lane C, Lane D
Secondary interaction: Ceiling zone
Sector scope: 1–2 sectors (rarely full stack)
Avoids drift floor unless breaking out of muted decay

Spacing Signature:
Wide spacing during burst (spacing expands)
Pre‑burst compression signature: tight spacing in Lane B
Post‑burst settling: spacing collapses back toward drift → expansion corridor
Typical spacing change: +5 to +10 units during burst

Actor Type: Cascade Actor

Often triggers ceiling‑test actors or purge actors afterward

Lane / Sector Scope:

 Actor Type: Drift Actor
Purpose / Role: Maintains drift corridors and forms stable drift floors.

Core Mechanics:
Creates slow, controlled motion inside drift corridors
Establishes the engine’s passive equilibrium zone
Forms drift floors through muted decay + symmetry
Maintains tight spacing during low‑velocity phases
Provides the “resting state” between bursts and cascades
Enables drift → expansion transitions when actors re‑enter
Stabilizes geometry after chaotic or high‑velocity events
Drift actors are the engine’s stabilizers. 

Lane / Sector Scope:
Primary lanes: Drift Floor + Lane A
Secondary interaction: Lane B (early expansion)
Sector scope: 1 sector (rarely more)
Avoids upper lanes unless transitioning into expansion
Drift actors operate at the bottom of the engine stack.

Spacing Signature:
Tight spacing (minimal variance)
Typical spacing: 1–3 units
Pre‑drift signature: muted decay + symmetry
Drift floor signature: repeated taps with minimal displacement
Post‑drift signature: spacing widens slightly as expansion begins.
Drift spacing is the tightest of all actor types.

Actor Type: Weaver Actor 
Purpose / Role: Stitches lanes, maintains corridor spacing, and keeps the engine structurally alive.

Core Mechanics: (Weaver actors are the engine’s structural maintainers.)
Maintains lane spacing across multiple lanes simultaneously
Rebuilds corridor symmetry after bursts, cascades, vacuums, or purges
Performs “lane stitching” — micro‑burst → micro‑drift → micro‑burst cycles
Resets inheritance without moving price directionally
Prevents engine collapse by maintaining structural continuity
Operates across sectors using 5‑spacing logic
Creates the engine’s “alive but not moving” behavior
Supports drift → expansion transitions by stabilizing mid‑range geometry

Lane / Sector Scope:
Primary lanes: Lane A, Lane B, Lane C
Secondary lanes: Lane D (only when stitching upper corridors)
Sector scope: 2–5 lanes per sector, often multiple sectors
Only maintains sectors that currently exist on the spread
Avoids drift floor unless rebuilding after purge or vacuum collapse
Weaver actors operate across entire corridor stacks, not single lanes. 

Spacing Signature:
Uses 5‑spacing units between lanes
Maintains consistent spacing across sectors
Pre‑weaver signature: chaotic spacing or broken geometry
Weaver signature: repeated micro‑taps with controlled spacing
Post‑weaver signature: stable corridor ready for expansion or drift
Weaver spacing is consistent, structural, and sector‑wide.
